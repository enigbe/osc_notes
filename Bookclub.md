## Book Club Proposal
> I think there are probably three to five books that separate the current you and the exceptional you

### Why?
1. A proven method for groups of people to deepen knowledge in a shared area of interest.
2. Successfully run clubs like Mastering Bitcoin by Btrust, and Software Internals by Eaton Phil prove helpful in:
    - sparking joy in learning
    - finding meaningful work
    - creating impactful writing & other digital artifacts
3. A desire to acquire deep, foundational knowledge in keys fields that impact Bitcoin
    - Graph Theory and Algorithms
    - Cryptography
    - Distributed Systems
    - Number Theory
    - Networking
    - Systems Programming
4. A desire to
contribute beyond the periphery of existing software systems in the space.
(and freedom to) build systems for ourselves.
5. To develop mechanical sympathy for the hardware we build on.

### Proposed System
1. Maintain a pool of proposed books to read. Anyone can contribute. Reading selections must be agreed upon through consensus (voting), but participation is voluntary and driven by interest
2. Books should ideally be 12–14 chapters long. This allows:

    - Completion in 6–7 weeks (2 chapters/week) or 12–14 weeks (1 chapter/week).
    - At each BitDevs meeting, chapter leads will guide the discussion. This can be formal, informal, presentation-based, or demo-based—entirely at their discretion.
    - Hard requirements:
        - We must connect what we learn directly to Bitcoin project implementations.
        - Sessions must be time-bound (details TBD).
3. Not necessarily limited to books. High-quality senior undergrad and/or graduate-level courses (e.g. Robert Sedgewick, Dan Boneh) could also suffice.

### Challenges
1. People don't like to read. Time is scarce and life is already hard.
2. Some topics are difficult to understand.
3. Lack of an expert or sufficiently advanced member can hinder discussion.
4. Tragedy of the commons:
    - One or two people carry all the weight.
    - Others stop attending, especially once the material becomes difficult.
    - Discussions become low-value because people haven't prepared.

### Benefits
1. Dive deeper into important projects.
2. Improve as software engineers. 
3. Expand the scope/scale of work we can take on or build.
4. Earn a sensible income - through skills or built systems.

### Feedback
1. Balancing the book club to include beginners.
2. Keeping reviews and discussions grounded in in-person meetings.
